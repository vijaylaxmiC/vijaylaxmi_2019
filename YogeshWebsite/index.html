About Me

Dance with your heart.And your feet will follow.

    • Bharatanatyam, the oldest form of Indian classical dances is comprised of music and rhythm.
    • A traditional, yet very much sophisticated to draw the attention of the art lovers with it's powerful 'Abhinaya' and 'nrutta' supplementing each other.

  • Mr. Yogesh Kumar, since his childhood was fascinated by this art form and it became a passion to him as he started learning the same. Rightly, he chose to become a professional dancer. Today, with his keen ability to understand and imbibe the nuances of the dance form, he has grown as one of the major performers of the younger generation artists.

  • Mr. Yogesh Kumar bagan his training under Smt K Brunda and at present he is under the tutelage of Karnataka Kalashree Shri Sathyanarayana Raju, a world renowned exponent of Bharatanatyam.

  • # A Doordarshan 'B' grade artist # 2nd best dancer in "Spirit Of Youth" festival held from Music academy, Chennai # Vidwat grade holder from Karnataka secondary education board #

  • • A regular performer- has widely performed in India and abroad, • A choreographer – Choreographed many beautiful numbers in Bharatanatyam repertoire and for other ballets too. • Teaches the aspirants in Women and child development department’ govt of Karnataka, in Nartana Yoga® (his own institution) and in ‘Samskruti Arts’.

1. Title :

Yogesh Kumar - Bharatanatyam Varnam, Disciple of Shri Satyanarayana Raju

Description : Varnam - Swami nanundan adimai, (Papanasham Shivam) Raga - Natakuranji Adi tala. Performed in music Academy Madras, for hcl concert. The famous varnam, penned down by the great vaggeyakara shri Papanasham Shivam.... "Swamiye nanundanadimi". Bhakti rasa is sthayi bhava here. The Bhakta prises lord Shiva in many ways. O' lord, every one knows that I am your dasa. The very purpose of my life is chanting your namamruta. I am eagerly waiting for your darshana. Here we witness the portrayal of the nandanar, to whom Shiva blessed with his darshana. Dance choreography, guru Smt. Narmada.

3. Title :

Yogesh Kumar - Bharatanatyam Devaranama, Disciple of Shri Satyanarayana Raju

Description : Devaranama -Hanumanta deva namo. Raga - Ragamalike Adi tala Hanumantha is always sought for his bhakti. This bhakti was his power to cross the vastness of the sea and reach Lanka, ravanasura's kingdom to find Seethamatha. And, once again, to save Seetha while she was try to hanging herself frustrated by ravana's crooked actions. Composed by shri purandara dasaru in purvikalyani and aditalam. Dance composition by Guru Bhanumathi.
